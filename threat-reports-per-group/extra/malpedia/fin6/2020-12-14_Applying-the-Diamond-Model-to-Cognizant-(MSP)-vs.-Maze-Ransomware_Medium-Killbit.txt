In our modern era, one of the most prevalent threats to the computing world is ransomware.
Ransomware is a form of malware that, once executed, encrypts files and the victim’s attached network shares (Columbus, 2019).
Once the files and connected network shares have become encrypted, the victim is presented with a ransom note that generally states for a payment to be made in exchange for the private key to decrypt the victim’s data.
